Malang Tourist Attractions

From colonial heritage to stunning nature, Malang offers a rich variety of attractions that combine history, culture, and adventure

Singosari Temple

Historic Temple

An ancient Hindu-Buddhist temple from the Singhasari Kingdom, located in Singosari district

Highlights

Ancient Ruins Temple Carvings Historical Atmosphere

Best Time: Morning for fewer crowds

Alun-Alun Malang

Urban Landmark

The city's central square surrounded by colonial architecture, mosques, and local cafes

Highlights

Street Food City Park Local Culture

Best Time: Evening when the lights and fountains turn on

Mount Bromo

Natural Landmark

One of Indonesia's most iconic volcanoes, accessible from Malang via Tumpang

Highlights

Sunrise View Sea of Sands Crater Trekking

Best Time: Sunrise for the best views

Coban Rondo Waterfall

Nature & Parks

A beautiful highland waterfall surrounded by lush forest, located near Batu

Highlights

Forest Trails Fresh Air Nature Exploration

Best Time: Early morning or after rain season

Travel Tips for Malang

Getting Around

  • Use angkot (public minibus) for cheap transport inside the city
  • Ride-hailing apps (Gojek/Grab) are widely available
  • Rent a motorbike or car for exploring Batu and Bromo
  • Malang has a train station with direct routes to Surabaya & Jakarta

Cultural Etiquette

  • Greet with a smile and polite "Salam"
  • Dress modestly when visiting temples and mosques
  • Bargaining is common in traditional markets
  • Respect nature sites, especially around Mount Bromo
